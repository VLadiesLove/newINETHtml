Victoria Lilti won first place (alongside another two teams) in our first-ever LaunchPad competition, for the idea: "Developing the Smallholders Market in Africa solving installation and maintenance problems."

The idea in short: Netafim offers great products (NetaKits) for small-scale farmers, and the smallholder market is huge worldwide (600 million farmers in Africa alone!) However, Netafim faces four main obstacles to tackling this market: farm remoteness, very low individual sale value, absence of local knowledge on drip, and insufficient Netafim training materials for people who are totally new to drip.

Victoria's idea is to develop the smallholder market in Africa using on-line end-to-end support that solves the problem of expensive visits to remote areas; helps you visualize, install, and maintain your Netafim small-holder drip irrigation kit; and can be accessed from a phone in real time.
